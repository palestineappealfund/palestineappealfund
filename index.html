The Humanitarian Crisis in Gaza

Gaza continues to face a catastrophic humanitarian emergency as of 2025. Entire neighborhoods remain in rubble, hospitals are overwhelmed, and essential supplies like food and electricity are scarce. Families endure dire circumstances, struggling to survive amidst ongoing challenges.

More than half of Gaza’s population are children, many of whom are displaced, injured, or traumatized. Schools, clinics, and homes lie in ruins, depriving countless children of education and stability.

Humanitarian aid struggles to reach those in need due to restricted access. Innocent civilians—mothers, fathers, and children—bear the devastating consequences of this ongoing crisis.

Famine in Gaza

Famine persists in Gaza as of 2025. Over 100,000 children remain at risk of malnutrition due to limited access to food supplies (Source: UN Report, 2025).

Our teams are responding — running two primary health clinics in Deir al-Balah and Khan Younis, treating hundreds of malnourished children each month and providing life-saving therapeutic food to children and mothers. However, aid blockages at borders continue to exacerbate the crisis.

This famine is man-made. It will only end with unrestricted humanitarian access and a sustained ceasefire.
